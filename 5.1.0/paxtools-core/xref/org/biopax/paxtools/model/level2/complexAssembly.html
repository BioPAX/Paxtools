3   /**
4    * A conversion interaction in which a set of physical entities, at least one
5    * being a macromolecule (e.g. protein, RNA, DNA), aggregate via non-covalent

7    * instance of the class complex (via a physicalEntityParticipant instance).
8    *
9    * <b>Comment:</b> This class is also used to represent complex disassembly.
10   * The assembly or disassembly of a complex is often a spontaneous process, in

12   * disassembly) should be specified via the SPONTANEOUS property.
13   *
14   * <b>Synonyms:</b> aggregation, complex formation
15   * Examples: Assembly of the TFB2 and TFB3 proteins into the TFIIH complex, and
16   * assembly of the ribosome through aggregation of its subunits.
17   * Note: The following are not examples of complex assembly: Covalent
18   * phosphorylation of a protein (this is a biochemicalReaction); the TFIIH
19   * complex itself (this is an instance of the complex class, not the
20   * complexAssembly class).
21   */
