3   /**
4    * Definition: An entity feature that represents the resulting physical entity subsequent to a cleavage or
5    * degradation event.
6    *
7    * Usage: Fragment Feature can be used to cover multiple types of modfications to the sequence of the physical
8    * entity:
9    * <ul>
10   * <li>    A protein with a single cleavage site that converts the protein into two fragments (e.g. pro-insulin
11   * converted to insulin and C-peptide). TODO: CV term for sequence fragment?  PSI-MI CV term for cleavage site?
12   * <li>    A protein with two cleavage sites that removes an internal sequence e.g. an intein i.e. ABC -&gt; A
13   * <li>    Cleavage of a circular sequence e.g. a plasmid.
14   * </ul>
15   * In the case of removal ( e.g. intron)  the fragment that is *removed* is specified in the feature location
16   * property. In the case of a "cut" (e.g. restriction enzyme cut site) the location of the cut is specified instead.
17   * Examples: Insulin Hormone
18   */
