6   /**
7    * Definition: A control interaction in which a physical entity (a catalyst) increases the rate of a conversion
8    * interaction by lowering its activation energy. Instances of this class describe a pairing between a catalyzing
9    * entity and a catalyzed conversion.
10   *
11   * Rationale: Catalysis, theoretically, is always bidirectional since it acts by lowering the activation energy.
12   * Physiologically, however, it can have a direction because of the concentration of the participants. For example,
13   * the oxidative decarboxylation catalyzed by Isocitrate dehydrogenase always happens in one direction under
14   * physiological conditions since the produced carbon dioxide is constantly removed from the system.
15   *
16   * Usage: A separate catalysis instance should be created for each different conversion that a physicalEntity may
17   * catalyze and for each different physicalEntity that may catalyze a conversion. For example,
18   * a bifunctional enzyme that catalyzes two different biochemical reactions would be linked to each of those
19   * biochemical reactions by two separate instances of the catalysis class. Also, catalysis reactions from multiple
20   * different organisms could be linked to the same generic biochemical reaction (a biochemical reaction is generic if
21   * it only includes small molecules). Generally, the enzyme catalyzing a conversion is known and the use of this
22   * class is obvious, however, in the cases where a catalyzed reaction is known to occur but the enzyme is not known,
23   * a catalysis instance can be created without a controller specified.
24   *
25   * Synonyms: facilitation, acceleration.
26   *
27   * Examples: The catalysis of a biochemical reaction by an enzyme, the enabling of a transport interaction by a
28   * membrane pore complex, and the facilitation of a complex assembly by a scaffold protein. Hexokinase -&gt; (The
29   * "Glucose + ATP -&gt; Glucose-6-phosphate +ADP" reaction). A plasma membrane Na+/K+ ATPase is an active transporter
30   * (antiport pump) using the energy of ATP to pump Na+ out of the cell and K+ in. Na+ from cytoplasm to extracellular
31   * space would be described in a transport instance. K+ from extracellular space to cytoplasm would be described in a
32   * transport instance. The ATPase pump would be stored in a catalysis instance controlling each of the above
33   * transport instances. A biochemical reaction that does not occur by itself under physiological conditions,
34   * but has been observed to occur in the presence of cell extract, likely via one or more unknown enzymes present in
35   * the extract, would be stored in the CONTROLLED property, with the CONTROLLER property empty.
36   */
