3   /**
4    * Definition: An interaction in which one entity regulates, modifies, or otherwise influences another. Two types of
5    * control interactions are defined: activation and inhibition.
6    *
7    * Comment: The targets of control processes (i.e. values of the controlled property) should be Interactions or
8    * Pathways, not physical entities. The physical entities are involved in processes,
9    * which are controlled.  The physical entities are not themselves controlled. For example,
10   * a kinase activating a protein is a frequent event in signaling pathways and is usually represented in signaling
11   * diagrams using an ‘activation’ arrow from the kinase to the substrate. The problem with this is that the substrate
12   * may not be active in other contexts. For this reason, BioPAX does not support these types of control or activation
13   * flow networks. In BioPAX, this information should be captured as the kinase catalyzing (via an instance of the
14   * Catalysis class) a reaction in which the substrate is phosphorylated.
15   *
16   * Synonyms: regulation, mediation
17   *
18   * Examples: A small molecule that inhibits a pathway by an unknown mechanism controls the pathway.
19   *
20   * Notes: Instances of Control can have multiple controller’s and controlled’s. Moreover,
21   * one Control instance can control another Control instance. The semantics of the use of these properties are as
22   * follows:
23   *
24   * Multiple separate controls controlling a conversion means that they control in parallel (e.g. different enzymes
25   * catalyzing the same reaction). Generally, their effect on the rate of the reaction is cumulative.
26   *
27   * A control with multiple controllers indicates a dependency between these controllers,
28   * typically meaning that both are required for the reaction to occur (e.g. a catalysis with an enzyme and a cofactor
29   * as controllers). Any further chaining of controls also implies dependency, for example allosteric inhibition of
30   * the aforementioned enzyme by a small molecule.
31   *
32   * Here is a pseudo-BioPAX representation of the examples above:
33   * rxn1 is a BiochemicalReaction
34   *
35   * cat1 is a Catalysis
36   * cat2 is a Catalysis
37   *
38   * mod1 is a Modulation
39   *
40   * enzyme1 is a Protein
41   * enzyme2 is a Protein
42   *
43   * cofactor1 is a SmallMolecule
44   * drug1 is a SmallMolecule
45   *
46   * cat1 has controlled rxn1
47   * cat2 has controlled rxn1 (Both cat1 and cat2 can catalyze rxn1, independently)
48   *
49   * cat1 has controller enzyme1
50   *
51   * cat2 has controller enzyme2
52   * cat2 has cofactor cofactor1 (both enzyme2 and cofactor1 is required for cat2 to occur)
53   *
54   * mod1 has controlled cat2
55   * mod1 has control-type INHIBITION_ALLOSTERIC
56   * mod1 has controller drug1 (drug1 should NOT be present for cat2 to occur)
57   *
58   * This structure is similar to disjunctive normal form (DNF) in Boolean logic. We could write this as: (enzyme1) OR
59   * (enzyme2 AND cofactor1 AND NOT drug1)
60   */
