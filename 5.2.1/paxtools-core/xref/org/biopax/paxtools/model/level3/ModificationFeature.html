3   /**
4    * Definition: A covalently modified feature on a sequence, relevant to an interaction,
5    * such as a post-translational modification. The difference between this class and BindingFeature is that this is
6    * covalent and BindingFeature is non-covalent.
7    *
8    * Rationale: In Biology, identity of DNA, RNA and Protein entities are defined around a wildtype sequence. Covalent
9    * modifications to this basal sequence are represented using modificaton features. Since small molecules are
10   * identified based on their chemical structure, not sequence, a covalent modification to a small molecule would
11   * result in a different molecule.
12   *
13   * Examples: A phosphorylation feature on a protein that enables the binding of an SH2 domain.
14   *
15   * Usagee: The added groups should be simple and stateless, such as phosphate or methyl groups and are captured
16   * by the modificationType controlled vocabulary. In other cases, such as covalently linked proteins,
17   * use CovalentBindingFeature instead. SmallMolecules can only have covalentBindingFeatures.
18   */
19  public interface ModificationFeature extends EntityFeature
20  {
21  	/**
22  	 * @return Description and classification of the feature.
23  	 */
24  	SequenceModificationVocabulary getModificationType();
25  
26  	/**
27  	 * @param featureType Description and classification of the feature.
28  	 */
29  	void setModificationType(SequenceModificationVocabulary featureType);
30  
31  }
