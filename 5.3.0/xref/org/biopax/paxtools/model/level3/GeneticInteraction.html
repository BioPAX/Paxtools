3   /**
4    * Definition : Genetic interactions between genes occur when two genetic perturbations (e.g. mutations) have a
5    * combined phenotypic effect not caused by either perturbation alone. A gene participant in a genetic interaction
6    * represents the gene that is perturbed. Genetic interactions are not physical interactions but logical (AND)
7    * relationships. Their physical manifestations can be complex and span an arbitarily long duration.
8    *
9    * Rationale: Currently,  BioPAX provides a simple definition that can capture most genetic interactions described in
10   * the literature. In the future, if required, the definition can be extended to capture other logical relationships
11   * and different, participant specific phenotypes.
12   *
13   * Example: A synthetic lethal interaction occurs when cell growth is possible without either gene A OR B,
14   * but not without both gene A AND B. If you knock out A and B together, the cell will die.
15   */
16  public interface GeneticInteraction extends Interaction
17  {
18  
19  	/**
20  	 * @return The phenotype quality used to define this genetic interaction e.g. viability.
21  	 */
22  	PhenotypeVocabulary getPhenotype();
23  
24  	/**
25  	 * @param phenotype The phenotype quality used to define this genetic interaction e.g. viability.
26  	 */
27  	void setPhenotype(PhenotypeVocabulary phenotype);
28  
29  	/**
30  	 * @return The score of an interaction e.g. a genetic interaction score.
31  	 */
32  	Score getInteractionScore();
33  
34  	/**
35  	 * @param interactionScore The score of an interaction e.g. a genetic interaction score.
36  	 */
37  	void setInteractionScore(Score interactionScore);
38  }
