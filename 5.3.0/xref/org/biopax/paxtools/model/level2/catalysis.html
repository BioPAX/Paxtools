5   /**
6    * A control interaction in which a physical entity (a catalyst) increases the
7    * rate of a conversion interaction by lowering its activation energy. Instances
8    * of this class describe a pairing between a catalyzing entity and a catalyzed
9    * conversion.
10   *
11   * <b>Comment:</b>  A separate catalysis instance should be created for each
12   * different conversion that a physicalEntity may catalyze and for each
13   * different physicalEntity that may catalyze a conversion. For example, a
14   * bifunctional enzyme that catalyzes two different biochemical reactions would
15   * be linked to each of those biochemical reactions by two separate instances of
16   * the catalysis class. Also, catalysis reactions from multiple different
17   * organisms could be linked to the same generic biochemical reaction (a
18   * biochemical reaction is generic if it only includes small molecules).
19   * Generally, the enzyme catalyzing a conversion is known and the use of this
20   * class is obvious. In the cases where a catalyzed reaction is known to occur
21   * but the enzyme is not known, a catalysis instance should be created without a
22   * controller specified (i.e. the CONTROLLER property should remain empty).
23   *
24   * <b>Synonyms:</b> facilitation, acceleration.
25   *
26   * <b>Examples:</b> The catalysis of a biochemical reaction by an enzyme, the
27   * enabling of a transport interaction by a membrane pore complex, and the
28   * facilitation of a complex assembly by a scaffold protein.
29   * Hexokinase -&gt; (The "Glucose + ATP -&gt; Glucose-6-phosphate +ADP" reaction).
30   * A plasma membrane Na+/K+ ATPase is an active transporter (antiport pump)
31   * using the energy of ATP to pump Na+ out of the cell and K+ in. Na+ from
32   * cytoplasm to extracellular space would be described in a transport instance.
33   * K+ from extracellular space to cytoplasm would be described in a transport
34   * instance. The ATPase pump would be stored in a catalysis instance
35   * controlling each of the above transport instances. A biochemical reaction
36   * that does not occur by itself under physiological conditions, but has been
37   * observed to occur in the presence of cell extract, likely via one or more
38   * unknown enzymes present in the extract, would be stored in the CONTROLLED
39   * property, with the CONTROLLER property empty.
40   */
41
